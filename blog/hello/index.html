Hello Internet. You don't know me, but I know you well.

Why I'm starting a blog after years of meaning to, and what you can expect from it.

personal

I’ve been a software engineer for over a decade. In that time, I’ve built things, broken things, and debugged things at 2 AM that I swore worked five minutes ago. There have been times when I felt like a genius, then felt dumb shortly after. I rode the Dunning-Kruger curve like a rollercoaster.

My career has had a lot of ups and downs, and I’ve learned a lot along the way. But for some reason, I haven’t shared much of it. A mix of overthinking, overengineering, procrastination and imposter syndrome has kept me from sharing a lot of what I’ve learned. I’m finally ready to change that.

Why blog?

The ideas I find most useful in my work tend to come from other engineers who took the time to share what they learned. I’ve been on the receiving end of that generosity for years, and it felt like it was time to contribute back.

There’s also a selfish reason. The probability that someone might read something I wrote and find it useful is a powerful motivator. Ideas flow into my mind just by thinking about how I could be useful to others. It’s a way to force myself to think deeply about the things I can contribute to the world.

What to expect

I plan to write about the things I actually deal with. Frontend architecture, CSS tricks, TypeScript patterns. Practical stuff, grounded in real code and real trade-offs.

I’m not going to pretend this is going to be very interesting for all readers. Some posts will be me experimenting with some niche thing nobody cares about. But I’m sure they all will be interesting to at least someone, and that’s enough for me. I can’t promise a schedule either. I’d rather write something worth reading occasionally than push out filler every week.

So, hello! Thanks for reading. Let’s see where this goes.
